Description

Albert Jose is a third-year Electronics & Communication Engineering student at Christ College of Engineering, Irinjalakuda, specialising in embedded systems, IoT, and hardware design. He builds Arduino and ESP8266-based systems using I2C, UART, and wireless protocols, ranging from autonomous navigation robots to cloud-connected environmental monitoring dashboards.

He is currently undergoing advanced IoT training at IIIT Kottayam as one of 150 selected participants under the PM-VIKAS scheme, a flagship Govt. of India skill-development initiative — the first time a higher education institution in India has implemented it. Outside coursework, his projects trace a steady progression: a sensor-driven maze-solving robot, an internet-connected weather station, and most recently a wireless motion-capture system linking an accelerometer straight to a Raspberry Pi.

He's open to internship and collaborative opportunities in embedded systems, IoT, and hardware design — reach out via the contact page.
